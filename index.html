Project Overview

The SolarShade360 is an innovative solar-adaptive shading system that automatically adjusts based on sunlight intensity. Our device combines advanced origami-inspired mechanisms with smart sensor technology to provide optimal shade coverage while maintaining environmental awareness.

Key Innovation

We've developed a Hoberman-Circle origami structure that expands and contracts based on real-time light measurements, providing dynamic shade coverage that adapts to changing environmental conditions throughout the day.

Why Spiral Design?

Our choice of spiral-based deployable structure is grounded in extensive research on optimizing deployment efficiency and minimizing shear stress during operation.

Research Foundation

Based on the research "Shear Between Straight Ribs in Spirally Stowed Sheets," we discovered that spiral folding patterns require less energy for deployment, making them ideal for our adaptive shading system. The spiral configuration offers several critical advantages:

Minimal Shear Stress

The spiral stowage pattern distributes stress evenly across ribs, preventing localized failure points that plague traditional folding mechanisms.

Smooth Deployment

Continuous curvature of the spiral eliminates sharp fold lines, enabling smoother transitions between stowed and deployed states.

Space Efficiency

Equiangular spiral folding patterns can save considerable space and provide a new approach to spatially deployable structures, achieving superior stowage ratios compared to traditional designs.

Mathematical Optimization

The spiral geometry follows an Archimedean spiral equation, allowing us to precisely control the deployment radius as a function of rotation angle. This mathematical relationship enables predictable, repeatable deployment cycles essential for long-term reliability.

Light Sensor Integration

BH1750 lightMeter;
const int LIGHT_THRESHOLD = 50;

void setup() {
  Wire.begin();
  if(!lightMeter.begin(BH1750::CONTINUOUS_HIGH_RES_MODE)) {
    Serial.println("BH1750 not detected!");
    while(1);
  }
}

Adaptive Motor Control

void loop() {
  float lux = lightMeter.readLightLevel();
  
  if (lux > LIGHT_THRESHOLD) {
    myStepper.step(-STEP_CHUNK);
    drawSunAnimation();
  } else {
    myStepper.step(STEP_CHUNK);
    drawMoonAnimation();
  }
}

Advanced Polyimide Film

We're investigating Kapton polyimide film with specialized coatings for our deployable shade structure, focusing on optimal light transmission while maintaining structural integrity.

Material Specifications

  • Film thickness: 10-50 Âµm for optimal mechanical properties
  • Variant: Kapton "FN" with fluoropolymer coating
  • Coatings: UV absorbers, anti-scratch topcoat, hydrophobic layer
  • Spectral filtering: Embedded UV/IR management layers
  • Reinforcement: Fine mesh skeleton for tear resistance

Performance Metrics

  • Initial visible light transmission: 70-90%
  • UV filtering: Short-wavelength absorption capability
  • Long-term degradation: 5-20% over years
  • Spectral range: 400-700 nm plus near-IR/UV
